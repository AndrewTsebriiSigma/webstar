## **LAW 1: DESIRE IS IMITATIVE** *(RenÃ© Girard)*

```
You don't want things because of the thing itself.
You want things because OTHERS want them.
The model (influencer, peer, hero) creates the desire.
```

**The Mechanism:**
- **External mediation:** We see someone distant (celebrity) wanting something â†’ we want it
- **Internal mediation:** Someone close (peer) wants something â†’ we want it â†’ they become rival
- **Metaphysical desire:** We don't want the object, we want to BE the model

**Implications:**
- Marketing doesn't create desire â€” it channels existing mimetic patterns
- Exclusivity works because others wanting what you can't have intensifies desire
- Community works because shared desire creates belonging
- Scarcity amplifies because it creates visible unfulfilled desire in others

## **LAW 2: SUCCESS COMPOUNDS (MATTHEW EFFECT)** *(Robert Merton)*

```
"For unto every one that hath shall be given..."
Small early advantages â†’ compound over time
Rich get richer. Famous get more famous.
Success breeds success.
```

**The Mechanism:**
1. Small initial edge (luck, timing, visibility)
2. Systems reward the already-successful
3. Gap widens exponentially
4. Without intervention, inequality increases

**Implications:**
- First-mover advantage is real but not because of being first â€” because of compounding
- Early momentum matters more than later perfection
- Distribution follows power laws, not normal curves
- The gap between top performers and average widens over time

## **LAW 3: WHAT SURVIVES, CONTINUES (LINDY EFFECT)** *(Nassim Taleb)*

```

```

**The Mechanism:**
- Every year of survival = evidence of antifragility
- Future expected lifetime â‰ˆ current age

**Implications:**
- Build on what has lasted (philosophy, psychology, human nature)
- Be skeptical of new trends until they prove themselves
- The timeless beats the timely
- Ancient wisdom (2500 years) will outlast modern frameworks (5 years)
